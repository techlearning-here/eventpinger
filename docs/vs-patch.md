# Eventpinger vs Patch

**Purpose:** Stakeholder-ready comparison of how the Eventpinger concept (see [`readme.md`](../readme.md) and [`business-idea.md`](./business-idea.md)) differs from [Patch](https://www.patch.com/)—a large US **hyperlocal news** network that also hosts **community event** listings and newsletters.

**Caveat:** Patch’s **product and monetization** evolve. Verify on their live properties. This document reflects **our** intended positioning, not a real-time audit of Patch.

---

## 1. Strategic framing

| | **Patch** | **Eventpinger (intended)** |
|---|-----------|----------------------------|
| **Core identity** | **Local news + journalism + ads**, with **community calendars** and neighbor-submitted events as one surface among many (crime, schools, obituaries, politics, etc.) | **Purpose-built** platform for **discovering, marketing, and converting** on **local activities and events**—especially **family, cultural, educational, and community** programming |
| **Geography** | **Neighborhood- and town-scoped** sites (Patch “edition” per area); strong **hyperlocal** editorial voice | **United States–wide** publishing; users discover by **their own** anchors and **up to three radius zones**, not a single Patch-style edition |
| **Discovery & filters** | Browse calendars and articles; event posts mixed into **news** context | **Anonymous** users filter on the **main page**; **signed-in** users get **saved** preferences, **dashboard** (matching-only), **rule/score-based** personalization (**B** in readme), **cultural tags**, kids’ ages, schedule fit |
| **Monetization** | **Advertising** and **media** model; events support **traffic** and **community engagement**, not typically a **ticketing marketplace** | **Organizer listing fees** (e.g., **first event free**) and/or **organizer subscription**; **public** discovery/notifications free—**ticket sales** stay on **organizer-chosen** platforms in the **initial** model |
| **Organizer tooling** | **Submit** an event to a local calendar; share into **newsletter** rotation | **Organizer accounts**, **stats** (views, interest aggregates, **registration link clicks**, etc.), directionally **leads → (optional) team chat → outbound signup/ticket URL**; **end of life** on listings and **DB purge** per policy |
| **Attendee engagement** | Read, share, maybe RSVP depending on listing; **no** unified **interest count** on every card as a first-class product primitive (Patch may vary by implementation) | **Aggregated interest count** on **every event card** and **detail page** (visible to **all**); **only logged-in** users add/remove interest |
| **Notifications** | **Email newsletters** (“Patch AM,” digests) and editorial **promotion** of picks | **Event notification subscriptions** driven by **user preferences**, **email + WhatsApp**, **immediate vs digest** tied to **match scoring**—not editor-curated as the default |
| **Data & privacy** | News and community platform norms | Explicit handling of **sensitive** profile fields (**kids**, **cultural identity**), **generic recommendations** opt-out, **no** sharing of **individual** family profiles with organizers (**aggregates only**) |

## 2. Where Eventpinger aims to be stronger *for your segments*

1. **Intent, not headlines** — Users come for **“what can my family do this weekend that fits us?”**, not for **local news** with events as a sidebar.
2. **Structured relevance** — **Three radius zones**, **per-zone categories**, **cultural/community tags**, and **scoring** beat a **flat calendar** for **long-tail** classes, temples, language schools, and camps.
3. **Commercial loop for organizers** — **Listing/subscription** aligns product success with **marketing ROI** (reach, leads, link clicks); Patch optimizes **readership and ads**, not **local event distribution** for long-tail hosts.
4. **Always-on demand** — **Subscriptions** (email/WhatsApp) for **new matching events**, not only **what an editor featured today**.
5. **Social proof on listings** — **Public interest counts** everywhere reinforce **traction** for small organizers.

---

## 3. Where Patch remains formidable

- **Trust and habit** in **specific towns** — “Check Patch for what’s on” is entrenched in many suburbs.
- **Editorial amplification** — A **human** pick in a **newsletter** can move **local** awareness without the organizer doing performance marketing.
- **Low friction to “post once”** for **free community** listings (from the organizer’s perspective)—even if **discovery** is noisy.

Eventpinger does not need to **out-news** Patch. It wins if organizers and families choose a **dedicated events** surface with **better matching** and **measurable intent** (**interest, clicks** to their **existing** registration stack)—not by replacing **Patch’s** news model.

---

## 4. One-line positioning

**Patch** is **hyperlocal news** with a **community calendar**. **Eventpinger** is a **nationwide events marketplace** built for **preference-driven discovery**, **saved dashboards**, **notifications**, **interest social proof**, and **organizer economics**—not for **reporting** the town.
