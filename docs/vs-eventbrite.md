# Eventpinger vs Eventbrite

**Purpose:** Stakeholder-ready comparison of how the Eventpinger concept (see [`readme.md`](../readme.md) and [`business-idea.md`](./business-idea.md)) differs from [Eventbrite](https://www.eventbrite.com/)—the largest self-serve ticketing and event-discovery brand in many US organizers’ minds.

**Caveat:** Eventbrite’s **features and pricing change**. Treat their site and current fee schedule as source of truth for live details. This document reflects **our** intended positioning, not a real-time product audit of Eventbrite.

---

## 1. Strategic framing

| | **Eventbrite** | **Eventpinger (intended)** |
|---|----------------|----------------------------|
| **Core identity** | General-purpose **ticketing + event pages** for a very wide range of events globally | **Local activities** marketplace: **discovery + marketing + leads + handoff** to the organizer’s **existing** signup/ticketing (e.g. **Eventbrite** links), with emphasis on **community, culture, education, and family** relevance |
| **Discovery** | Search, browse, categories, recommendations, **paid promotion** for organizers | **Profile-first** relevance (zip/radius, **kids’ ages**, **cultural tags**, interests, **schedule windows**), **score-based** ranking, **default event-notification subscriptions** |
| **Notifications** | Email and app experiences familiar to ticket buyers; not centered on **WhatsApp** for *new matching listings* | **Email** and **WhatsApp** as **event notification** channels (user-selected), plus **immediate vs digest** rules tied to match score |
| **Organizer economics** | Strongly **per-ticket** (plus **organizer subscriptions** / promo products—verify current model) | **Per-event listing fees** **and/or** **organizer subscription** plans (organizer’s choice by volume), **first event free** on per-event track—**listing-led** revenue, not **take rate** on ticket sales at initial launch; **public** not charged for discovery/notifications |
| **Listing lifecycle** | Long-lived **historical event pages** (SEO, receipts, organiser history) | Organizer-set **end of life**; **purge from database** after that time (with **retention** carve-outs if **future** on-platform payments exist) |
| **Journey story** | Checkout, organizer tools, some messaging/contact | **Discovery → interest/leads → (optional) chat → outbound registration URL**; **checkout** can stay on **Eventbrite** or other tools organizers already use |

---

## 2. Where Eventpinger aims to be stronger *for your segments*

1. **Hyper-relevance** — Matching **multiple children’s ages**, **cultural/ethnic/religious alignment**, and **when** families can actually attend—not only “events near you.”
2. **Ongoing demand** — **Event notification subscriptions** (by category, tags, area) so organizers reach people who **opted in** to that intent, not only one-off page visits.
3. **Channels communities already use** — **WhatsApp** alongside email for alerts (where policy allows), aligned with how many local organizers and families already coordinate.
4. **Affordable trial for hosts** — **First event free** lowers risk for small temples, studios, and volunteers compared to always paying full listing/ticket economics from day one.
5. **Privacy posture for sensitive fields** — Clear use of **kids + cultural** data for personalization; **generic recommendations** opt-out; **no individual family profiles sold** to organizers—**aggregates only** where insights are shown.

---

## 3. Where Eventbrite remains formidable (honest view)

- **Trust and habit** — Buyers and organizers **know** the brand; **payouts**, **refunds**, and **scale** are battle-tested.
- **Ticketing depth** — Seating, complex tiers, enterprise-ish cases—hard to replicate quickly.
- **SEO and inventory** — Massive **catalog** of past and future events drives **organic discovery** for generic queries.

Eventpinger does not win by claiming “we replace Eventbrite for every concert.” It wins by **owning a niche** (local, cultural, family, community) with **better fit**, **better notification loop**, and **clear organizer ROI**—if execution and trust keep pace.

---

## 4. One-line positioning

**Eventbrite** sells **tickets and event pages to everyone**. **Eventpinger** aims to sell **relevant local visibility + intent signals** (and **traffic** to the organizer’s **existing** ticket page)—using **family/culture-aware** defaults, **email/WhatsApp** subscriptions, and **listing/subscription** economics rather than **replacing** Eventbrite’s checkout for most hosts.
